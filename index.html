Property Overview

Nestled at 35 Longwestgate, Scarborough, United Kingdom, The Old Town Cottage is a charming historic mid-terrace property spanning three floors. Thoughtfully maintained over the years, this home offers a comfortable and inviting atmosphere where every detail has been carefully attended to.

  • Layout: Two main bedrooms (a first-floor double and a second-floor twin arrangement) plus a fully equipped bathroom (bath, shower, WC, and basin).
  • Living & Kitchen: Enjoy a tidy kitchen/diner with modern appliances alongside a comfortable sitting room featuring an electric fire, Smart TV, WiFi, and a curated collection of books and games.
  • Outdoor Space: A spacious decking area and garden invite you to relax and savor alfresco meals.

With thoughtful extras such as a highchair and travel cot available on request, and a pet-friendly policy (one well-behaved dog allowed), check-in is from 4:00 pm and check-out by 10:00 am.

Charming Accommodation

The cottage comfortably hosts up to four guests in rooms designed for quality and comfort:

  • Double Bedroom: Spacious and elegantly furnished on the first floor.
  • Twin Bedroom: Enjoy lovely views through charming roof windows.

The well-appointed bathroom features a comfortable bath, a separate shower, and contemporary fixtures.

Refined Living Spaces & Amenities

Our kitchen/diner is equipped with modern appliances that blend functionality with a well-cared-for charm:

  • Electric oven and induction hob
  • Microwave and premium coffee machine
  • Spacious fridge/freezer combo
  • Washing machine and dishwasher

The cozy sitting room—complete with an electric fireplace, Smart TV, high-speed WiFi, and a carefully curated selection of literature and games—creates a relaxed, home-like ambiance.

Outdoor Elegance & Additional Features

Step outside to our serene decking area—a peaceful garden space where you can enjoy alfresco dining or simply unwind by the sea.

Explore Scarborough

  • Scarborough Castle (≈300 m): Step back in time and explore the ancient ramparts of Scarborough Castle for stunning panoramic views.
  • Scarborough Fair (≈550 m): Stroll over to Scarborough Fair and immerse yourself in the vibrant local market atmosphere.
  • Scarborough Pleasure Steamers (≈550 m): Embark on a scenic cruise with the Scarborough Pleasure Steamers and enjoy the coastal breeze.
  • Freddie Gilroy and the Belsen Stragglers (≈600 m): Discover local lore at the Freddie Gilroy and the Belsen Stragglers monument, rich in history.
  • Scarborough Beach (≈650 m): Relax on the golden sands and take a refreshing dip at the picturesque Scarborough Beach.
  • St Martin on the Hill (≈650 m): Climb to St Martin on the Hill to enjoy breathtaking views over Scarborough and the sea.
  • Scarborough Museums Trust (≈650 m): Immerse yourself in the local heritage and art at the Scarborough Museums Trust.
  • St Mary’s Church (≈700 m): Admire the timeless architecture and serene atmosphere at St Mary’s Church.
  • Anne Bronte's Grave (≈700 m): Pay tribute to literary history with a visit to Anne Bronte’s Grave in a peaceful setting.
  • Peasholm Park (≈850 m): Feed the ducks and experience the tranquil oriental gardens at Peasholm Park.
  • Scarborough Train Station (≈900 m): Easily connect to regional destinations from the conveniently located Scarborough Train Station.
  • Durham Tees Valley Airport (≈99.2 km): Catch your flight with ease at Durham Tees Valley Airport, just a short drive from Scarborough.
